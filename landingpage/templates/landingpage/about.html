Our mission is to efficiently quantify opinion in a dynamic way.

A couple of months ago, Unibursal, this idea of a stock exchange-esque marketplace for opinions was just a crazy idea. Don't get us wrong, it's still just as crazy, but since then the two of us have been building, validating our idea with UVA students and other smart people, and building some more. Our mantra boils down to five statements:
(beware some technical language below)

01 the stock market works because people buy the stocks they think will go up, and sell the ones they think will go down.

02 These perceptions are based on an underlying: sometimes, it’s cash flow (stocks), sometimes it’s technological innovation (cryptos), and sometimes it’s just other people’s opinion.

03 The stock and crypto markets can be seen as a tool that ranks stocks and currencies on value by market cap.

04 Some opinions are more invested than others - a single like or upvote/downvote system isn’t enough to accurately rank people’s opinion.

05 People care about the opinions of people that they relate to.

Putting that together, we created assets whose demand/supply fundamentals come from people's opinions on them: those that rank high in public opinion should see their token price increase relative to the others that rank lower in public opinion. That way, we can get a quantitative measurement of the underlying opinion - not many people in the financial sector understand or care yet about quantifying opinion, and we're the first to implement it. We also get that there's always going to be lukewarm opinions right beside ones that are ride-or-die, and we don't think they should both be weighted equally. Turning the old voting/ranking mechanism into a market-based one means that that isn't a problem anymore - you put as much skin in the game as you want to. And finally, we understand that not everyone cares what everyone else thinks. That's why we're exclusive to UVA - we don't really care what people from other schools think about our classes, our culture, our social scene, and our community. Fuck Tech.
